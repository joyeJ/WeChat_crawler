If you want to get better at focusing, practice. Meditation is a great way to do. Count your breaths 1 through 10 (inhale, one, exhale, two, inhale three….)  and then start over when you get to 10. Every time you realize you have been distracted, bring your attention back to the breath. It’s hard to do consistently without getting distracted – try it, you’ll see -- but will get easier the more you do it, and this kind of brain training has been shown to have a demonstrable effect on our ability to focus. This is the best advice I can give you.</em></span></p><p style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; box-sizing: border-box;">大脑就像人的肌肉。想要保持专注，就需要不断的练习。冥想就是一个特别好的练习方式。你也可以数自己的呼吸（吸气，一，呼气，二，吸气，三……），数到十之后再从一开始重新数。每次发现自己分心的时候，要把注意力拉回来。一直做而不分心很难，不过尝试一下，你会发现，你做得次数越多，它就会变得越容易。这种注意力训练方法已经在提高人们的专注力方面取得显著效果。</p><p style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; box-sizing: border-box;"><br style="box-sizing: border-box;"/></p></section></section></section><section class="" style="   box-sizing: border-box; " powered-by="xiumi.us"><section class="" style=" text-align: center; margin-top: 10px; margin-bottom: 10px;  box-sizing: border-box; "><img class=" img_loading" data-ratio="0.9723756906077348" data-w="181" data-src="http://mmbiz.qpic.cn/mmbiz_jpg/yCOiaCWZEHHHWiaDvst0YAw8ySm9uVUUOgb5I7DLulqRNDtf3A0xJlcyPfVuDhwVEE1OR5usAhYibibzkWEqvnGiaNA/0?wx_fmt=jpeg" style="max-width: 100%; vertical-align: middle; box-sizing: border-box; width: 181px !important; height: 176px !important;" src="data:image/gif;base64,iVBORw0KGgoAAAANSUhEUgAAAAEAAAABCAYAAAAfFcSJAAAADUlEQVQImWNgYGBgAAAABQABh6FO1AAAAABJRU5ErkJggg=="/></section></section><section class="" style="   box-sizing: border-box; " powered-by="xiumi.us"><section class="" style="   box-sizing: border-box; "><section class="" style="box-sizing: border-box;"><p style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; box-sizing: border-box;"><span style="color: rgb(136, 136, 136);"><em>The science shows us there are more extreme things you might do – like put on noise cancelling headphones, or sit in a quiet room when you are reading. There is a young neuroscientist at NYU named Michael Halassa who works with ADHD patients. And he has discovered some interesting things about the way the brain’s attentional circuits work. The prefrontal cortex has sometimes been compared to a “track switcher” – it’s this part of the brain that controls what sensory information gets priority to travel to the areas of the brain that register the things we are consciously aware of.</em></span></p><p style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; box-sizing: border-box;">科学证明在排除干扰方面人们可以做更多辅助性的事情——比如说阅读的时候戴上降噪耳机，或者坐在一个安静的房间里。在纽约大学有一个年轻的神经学家Michael Halassa，跟ADHD（注意缺陷多动障碍）病人一起工作。他发现了一些关于大脑注意力神经网络运行的有意思的事情。前额叶皮层有时被比作“轨道切换器”——正是大脑的这一部分控制着感官信息优先到达记录着我们意识的大脑区域。</p><p style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; box-sizing: border-box;"><br/></p><p style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; box-sizing: border-box;"><span style="color: rgb(136, 136, 136);"><em>Halassa, however, has found that some patients with ADHD have deficits in their brains that make it harder for them to drown out irrelevant sensory noise. They might have no problem paying attention to John in a dimly lit quiet rooms. But they can’t focus when surrounded by other sensory stimuli and they don’t know why. Others with ADHD have problems with their executive functions. The track switching director doesn’t work so well. For them, this solution might not work as well. But for the rest of us, even those of us without ADHD, it’s useful to know.</em></span></p><p style="margin: 0px; padding: 0px; box-sizing: border-box;">Halassa发现患有注意缺陷多动障碍的病人大脑有缺陷导致他们很难排除其他无关噪音的干扰。对他们来说，在一个安静的昏暗的房间里注意到约翰可能没有问题，但是当被其他感觉刺激包围的时候，他们是无法集中精力的，并且不知道其中原因。其他患有注意缺陷多动障碍的人在执行力方面有问题。他们的轨道切换器不如正常人运行的顺利。因此对他们来说，这个方法可能不合适。但是对我们来说，即使对并不患有注意缺陷多动障碍的我们来说，知道这些也是有益的。</p><p style="margin: 0px;
